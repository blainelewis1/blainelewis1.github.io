Resume

I'm Blaine Lewis and a Master's student at the University of Waterloo. I study Human Computer Interaction supervised by Professor Daniel Vogel. My research is focused on helping users become more efficient through the design of interaction techniques.

My previous projects have studied how increasing effort impacts expertise development. I have also designed new interaction techniques for improving hotkey use. The underlying principles of interaction techniques are more important to me than the interaction technique itself. I'm especially interested in leveraging research from areas such as cognitive psychology and behavioural economics for developing new interaction techniques.

As my research matures I want to better understand how we should evaluate interaction techniques and move away from the focus on time and errors.
